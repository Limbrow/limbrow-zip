Further properties
f ∘ f = Id
Self-inverse. f(f(x)) = x for all x ≠ 0. The function is its own inverse — applying it twice returns the original value.
f(−x) = −f(x)
Odd symmetry. The graph is symmetric through the origin — the two branches are each other's rotation by 180°.
f ′(x) = −1/x²
Strictly decreasing on each branch. The curve always falls — on (0,+∞) and on (−∞,0) separately.
Conceptual reading Casa Axis × Limbrow

Casa Axis is the house of axes. The curve lives inside this house forever — turning from vertical to horizontal in a single, continuous motion, always approaching the walls but never touching them. The axes are not boundaries. They are asymptotes: directions the function inhabits as a permanent state of approach.

Limbrow is the limit. Not a destination — a tendency. The notation lim x → ∞ does not describe arrival; it describes the condition of infinite approach.

The function f(x) = 1/x diverges where you expect a center and vanishes where you expect an end. Its asymptotes are its identity.
